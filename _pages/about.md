Hello there! I'm Gabe Guo, an aspiring happy scientist. I am interested in designing mathematically principled deep generative models. 

Background
=====
I am a Computer Science PhD student at Stanford University. My research is proudly supported by the U.S. Government [Department of Energy's Computational Science Graduate Fellowship](https://www.krellinst.org/csgf/). I am advised by [Stefano Ermon](https://cs.stanford.edu/~ermon/), and work closely with [Jose Blanchet](https://joseblanchet.com/).

I graduated from Columbia University's School of Engineering and Applied Science in 2024, with a B.S. degree in Computer Science. There, I was an undergraduate research assistant in the [Creative Machines Lab](https://www.creativemachineslab.com/) at Columbia University under [Hod Lipson](https://www.hodlipson.com/), the Billinge Group under [Simon Billinge](https://billingegroup.github.io/), and the ZGroup under [Richard Zemel](https://www.cs.columbia.edu/~zemel/). I additionally learned irreplacable lessons in both research and teaching from [Tony Dear](https://www.engineering.columbia.edu/faculty/tony-dear). Prior to that, I was a high school and undergraduate research assistant at the Embedded Sensors and Computing Lab under [Wenyao Xu](https://cse.buffalo.edu/~wenyaoxu/).
